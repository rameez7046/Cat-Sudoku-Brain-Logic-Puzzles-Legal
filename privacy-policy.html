Privacy Policy

How Meowdoku handles your data

Meowdoku is a cat-placement puzzle game published by MindBlow Games. This page explains, in plain language, exactly what the app does and doesn't do with your information.

Last updated July 13, 2026  ·  Applies to the Meowdoku Android app and any future iOS release

01Scope

This Privacy Policy covers the Meowdoku app ("App," "we," "us") made by MindBlow Games. It applies wherever the App is distributed, including Google Play. By installing or playing Meowdoku, you agree to the practices described here. If you don't agree, please don't use the App.

03Information we collect

Stored only on your device

Meowdoku itself makes no network requests and has no server to send data to. The App stores a small amount of information locally on your device (via Android's standard app-preferences storage) purely so the game remembers where you left off:

  • Your current level number and puzzle progress
  • Whether you've accepted this policy (so we don't ask twice)
  • Your Music, Sound, Speak, and Vibration toggle choices

None of this leaves your device, and none of it identifies you personally. Uninstalling the App deletes it.

Collected by third-party SDKs (planned)

We intend to support the App with advertising and, potentially, analytics. Once those SDKs are integrated, the providers we choose (for example, Google AdMob for ads or Firebase Analytics) may independently collect:

  • Your device's advertising ID and general device information (model, OS version, language, approximate region)
  • Ad interaction data — which ads were shown, tapped, or completed
  • Coarse app-usage analytics — which screens or features are used and how often, and crash/diagnostic reports

This collection is performed by the third party's own SDK under its own privacy policy, not by code we operate ourselves. We'll keep the table in Section 2 and our Play Console Data Safety form updated as soon as any such SDK ships.

04How we use it

  • To run the game — restoring your saved level, puzzle state, and settings when you reopen the App.
  • To show and measure ads (once enabled) — selecting and reporting on ads through our advertising partner's systems.
  • To understand and improve the App (once analytics is enabled) — seeing which levels or features are popular, and finding crashes to fix.

We do not sell your information, and we do not use it for anything beyond operating, measuring, and improving Meowdoku.

05Advertising & your advertising ID

Android provides a resettable, user-controlled advertising ID instead of a permanent device identifier, specifically so you can limit how it's used for ads. Once Meowdoku serves ads, you can at any time:

  • Opt out of personalized ads — Android Settings → Google → Ads → "Opt out of Ads Personalization"
  • Reset your advertising ID — Android Settings → Google → Ads → "Reset advertising ID"

We will never combine your advertising ID with personally identifying information, and we won't use it for anything other than the advertising and measurement purposes disclosed here.

06Sharing & third parties

We don't share information with third parties for their own independent marketing purposes. The only sharing that happens is with the service providers named above (advertising and, if enabled, analytics platforms), strictly so they can perform the function we've engaged them for — showing/measuring ads or reporting usage analytics. Those providers process data under their own privacy policies; for Google's services, that's Google's Privacy Policy and its How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services disclosure.

We may also disclose information if required by law, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of MindBlow Games, our players, or the public.

07Children's privacy

Meowdoku is a general-audience puzzle game and is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If the App is ever made available to a child-directed or mixed audience under Google Play's Families Policy, we will:

  • Serve ads only through advertising partners and mediation platforms that are self-certified under the Google Play Families Ads SDK program
  • Avoid serving personalized ads to users known to be children
  • Comply with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and equivalent regional laws

A parent or guardian who believes their child has provided us with personal information may contact us using the details in Section 11 to have it reviewed or removed.

08Your rights & choices

Depending on where you live (for example, under the GDPR in the EEA/UK or the CCPA/CPRA in California), you may have the right to:

  • Ask what information is held about you and request a copy of it
  • Ask us to correct or delete it
  • Object to, or opt out of, certain processing, including personalized advertising
  • Withdraw any consent you've given, at any time

Because Meowdoku itself stores everything locally, the simplest way to exercise most of these rights is directly on your device: clearing the App's storage or uninstalling it removes your local save data immediately. For anything held by our advertising or analytics partners, use the controls in Section 5, or contact us and we'll help direct your request.

California residents: we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising in the way CCPA defines "sale," beyond the third-party ad measurement described in Section 5–6. You may still contact us to make a specific request.

09Security & retention

Local save data lives in Android's sandboxed app storage, protected by your device's own security. Since Meowdoku has no backend server, there is no MindBlow Games database of player data to secure or breach. Data collected by our advertising/analytics partners is retained according to their own retention schedules; local save data is retained on your device until you clear the App's storage or uninstall it.

10Changes to this policy

If we change how Meowdoku handles data — most notably, when we turn on advertising or analytics — we'll update this page and the "Last updated" date above, and update our Google Play Data Safety declaration to match. Significant changes will also be reflected in the App's in-game Settings, where this policy is always one tap away.
